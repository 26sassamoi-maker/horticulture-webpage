About the Department

The Taconic Horticulture Department combines hands-on learning with real-world skills in plant science, greenhouse management, and agricultural leadership. This website is designed by the in-house CTE IT team to provide a professional, approachable, and user-friendly online presence for staff, students, and the community.

Our goals are to:

  • Present a professional and welcoming image of the department
  • Make information easy to find through clear navigation and structure
  • Highlight programs, services, and seasonal activities

Student Activities

Students in the Taconic Horticulture program participate in a variety of hands-on activities that build technical skills, teamwork, and responsibility.

  • Greenhouse planting, watering, and plant care
  • Floral design projects and arrangements
  • Vegetable and herb production for seasonal sales
  • Landscape design and maintenance projects
  • FFA competitions, leadership events, and community service

FFA Program

The Taconic FFA chapter gives students the opportunity to develop leadership, career skills, and community connections through agricultural education.

  • Participation in local, state, and national FFA events
  • Career Development Events (CDEs) related to horticulture and agriculture
  • Community plant sales and service projects
  • Leadership roles within the chapter

Website Project Overview

This website is designed and developed by the Taconic CTE IT department as an internal project to support the Horticulture Department. The goals are to provide a stylish, easy-to-use, and informative resource for staff, students, and the public.

Scope & Objectives

  • Present a professional and approachable online presence
  • Make information easy to find through clear navigation and structure
  • Highlight programs, services, and seasonal activities
  • Optimize for desktop computer use
